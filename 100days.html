100 Days Challenge

I started this challenge on September 23 with no clear ideas of what to do. Initially, I began with a simple routine: waking up, brushing my teeth, washing my face, meditating 🧘, doing warm-up exercises, working out in my room 🏋️‍♀️, and making videos of my workouts (and all the sweating!). Afterward, I’d take a shower 🚿, change clothes, and write in my journal 📓.

I also committed to posting daily on YouTube and Instagram 📹📸. However, after a few days, unexpected work at night disrupted my routine. Sometimes editing took too much time, but I still managed to upload videos the next morning or afternoon.

On the 19th day, I had to go somewhere unexpectedly and came home late 🕒. I tried doing some workout but felt dizzy with vertigo, so I skipped that day 😵‍💫. The next day was Dashain festival 🎉, and I had to go to my uncle's house. I stayed there for 2–3 days, spending time with my family and friends 🏡❤️. During this period, I couldn’t find time to make or upload videos.

Once I returned, I resumed making videos, but I had to catch up on editing for the 19th to 23rd days. I continued recording videos, but uploading them on time became difficult ⏳.

By October 22 (30 days), I finished writing my journal 📒. On that same day, my data pack ran out 📵, so I decided to pause and restart from October 23 (31st day). On that day, I looked at my progress from Day 1 to Day 31, and I was amazed at the changes I noticed in myself! ✨ It felt like real progress.

However, the day went by doing housework, crop cutting 🌾, and managing other responsibilities. Over the next few days, I got busy again with household tasks. Weeks later, Tihar festival arrived 🪔, and I was occupied with celebrations and family time. After Tihar, work piled up again, and even on free days, I needed rest to recharge for the next day’s work 💼.

On November 20, I planned to start a 40-day challenge, but plans changed as I had to travel with friends 🚗. Instead, I decided to take on a 30-day challenge in December. During this time, I also worked on creating a website in 2–3 days, with help from ChatGPT 💻. I wrote 80% of the code myself and used ChatGPT for the remaining 20%. ChatGPT made so many mistakes 🤖⚠️, but I was able to fix them.

In the end, I wasn’t able to complete my 100-day challenge 💔. I gave it my best, but I struggled with consistency. Along the way, I faced anxiety, symptoms of stress, and other challenges (even felt like black magic was involved!) 😔.

Despite failing, I am proud that I tried my best 💪. This experience has taught me valuable lessons, and I am ready to set new goals for December 🎯.

Thank you for reading! Check out my December goals, and let’s keep growing together! 🌟
